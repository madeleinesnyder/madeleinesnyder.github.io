Science

As a neuroscientist I am passionate about understanding how agents with difference affordances build successful behavioral narratives. I am currently a doctoral researcher in the NeuroBat Lab at UC Berkeley, where my work centers on the neural code that supports navigation in multi-agent environments.

Selected publications:

Investigating the stability of the hippocampal code across days pioneering wireless 1P calcium in freely flying bats.

Project on the representation of the human experimenter in the bat hippocampus during a classical spatial reward task (in press).
